Peer Response #2

In response to https://www.my-course.co.uk/mod/forum/discuss.php?d=147482

Peer Response

Hi Mohammad. I enjoyed reading your post.

You mention that Abi should just provide the full results of his research to the manufacturer, and that afterwards he cannot be held responsible for what the manufacturer does with that data. However, data needs to be interpreted and translated for use by stakeholders. Rucker & Rucker (2016) makes a strong argument that researchers who are not involved in the interpretation of their research may be complicit in the mis-use of that research.

A particularly infamous example of this comes from the case of the Sugar Research Foundation, which often took data from researchers and interpreted it in ways which minimized the harms associated with sugar consumption (Kearns, 2016).

I would be curious to hear your thoughts on how far researchers need to go in order to combat misuse of their findings.

References

Kearns, C. E. et al. (2016) Sugar Industry and Coronary Heart Disease Research: A Historical Analysis of Internal Industry Documents. JAMA internal medicine. [Online] 176 (11), 1680–1685.

Rucker, R. B. & Rucker, M. R. (2016) Nutrition: ethical issues and challenges. Nutrition research (New York, N.Y.). [Online] 36 (11), 1183–1192.
